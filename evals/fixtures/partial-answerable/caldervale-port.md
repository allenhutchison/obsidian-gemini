# Caldervale Port

Caldervale is the river port at the downstream end of the [[Marrowfen Rail
Line]]. It sits where the Calder river widens enough for shallow-draft barges,
and it has been a transshipment point for upland goods for over a century.

## Layout

The port has three wharves. The North Wharf handles slate brought down by the
rail line; the Middle Wharf handles general freight; and the South Wharf is
reserved for the barge operators' own maintenance. A single steam crane serves
the North Wharf, installed when the rail line opened.

## Trade

The dominant trade through Caldervale is slate, but the port also ships milled
timber and, in season, the upland wool clip. Goods arriving by barge from
downstream — mainly coal and manufactured tools — are carried up the rail line
to Marrowfen on the return working.

## Governance

The port is administered by the Caldervale Wharf Board, a body of five elected
members who set wharf fees and maintain the common infrastructure. The Board
meets monthly and publishes its fee schedule each spring.
