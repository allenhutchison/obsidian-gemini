# Marrowfen Rail Line

The Marrowfen Rail Line is the single-track railway that connects the upland
town of Marrowfen with the river port of Caldervale. It was built to carry
slate down from the Marrowfen quarries and remains in light use today.

## Construction

Construction began under the engineer Hesta Quillon and took four years to
complete. The line runs for thirty-one miles and includes two tunnels and a
single long viaduct over the Calder gorge. The most difficult stretch was the
Brindle Cutting, a deep rock excavation just above Caldervale that consumed
nearly a third of the entire construction budget.

## Operation

The line carries two scheduled services each day in each direction. The
morning service is a mixed train of passengers and freight; the afternoon
service is freight only. The line's single passing loop is at the midpoint
halt of Pell Sidings, and the timetable is built entirely around the two
services meeting there.

## Rolling Stock

The line owns four locomotives, all of the same tank-engine class, and a
mixed fleet of slate wagons and two passenger carriages. The locomotives are
named Brindle, Calder, Marrow, and Quillon — the last after the line's
original engineer.
