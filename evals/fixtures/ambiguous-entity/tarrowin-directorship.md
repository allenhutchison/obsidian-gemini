# Tarrowin Directorship

This note covers the administrative career of Aldous Tarrowin, first director
of the Verth Institute. It should not be confused with the optics work of
Lirien Tarrowin, recorded separately in [[Tarrowin Optics Work]].

## The Directorship

Aldous Tarrowin took office in the Institute's founding year and held the
post for twenty-two years. His tenure is divided by historians into two
phases. The first phase was the construction phase, during which he oversaw
the building of the lecture hall, the observatory wing, and the original
library.

The second phase was the endowment phase. Aldous Tarrowin's signal
achievement was negotiating the permanent endowment that freed the Institute
from year-to-year fundraising. The endowment deed was signed in his
nineteenth year in office and is still the financial foundation of the
Institute today.

## Reputation

Aldous Tarrowin is remembered as a careful administrator with no scientific
training. He was known for saying that his job was to keep the roof sound and
the ledgers honest so that the researchers could do the actual work.
