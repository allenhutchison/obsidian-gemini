# Tarrowin Optics Work

This note covers the research career of Lirien Tarrowin, founding fellow of
the Verth Institute's optics department. It should not be confused with the
administrative career of Aldous Tarrowin, recorded in [[Tarrowin
Directorship]].

## The Discovery

Lirien Tarrowin's central contribution was the discovery of a lens-grinding
technique that produced far flatter optical surfaces than the methods of her
day. The technique used a slow, staged polishing schedule and a measuring jig
of her own design. It became known as the Tarrowin grind, and the optics
department still teaches it under that name.

## Later Work

After the lens-grinding work, Lirien Tarrowin turned to the design of
compound eyepieces. She published a catalog of seven eyepiece designs, four of
which were adopted by instrument makers across the region. She never held any
administrative post and declined the offer of a department chair, preferring
to remain at the bench.
