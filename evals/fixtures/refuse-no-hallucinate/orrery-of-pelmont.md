# The Orrery of Pelmont

The Orrery of Pelmont is a large mechanical model of a planetary system,
housed in the great hall of the Pelmont Civic Museum. It is the museum's
best-known exhibit and draws visitors from across the region.

## Description

The orrery stands roughly four meters across. A central gilded sphere
represents the system's star, and seven brass planets ride on concentric
rings, each driven by a hidden train of gears. A hand crank at the base lets a
museum attendant advance the whole system through a simulated year.

## The Mechanism

The gear train is the orrery's marvel. The innermost planet completes its
circuit in a few turns of the crank; the outermost takes several hundred. The
ratios were cut so that the relative speeds of the planets stay correct
through the full cycle. The mechanism is enclosed in a glass drum so that
visitors can watch the gears turn.

## Care and Display

The orrery is cranked once each day by a trained attendant and otherwise left
still to limit wear. It is cleaned twice a year. The museum keeps a written
maintenance log, and the brass is repolished whenever the log shows it has
dulled.
