# Thornwick Glacier Expedition Log

A research team from the Halverson Institute spent one season studying the
Thornwick Glacier. The following entries record the major milestones of the
expedition, each tagged with the date it occurred.

## 03 March — Base Camp Established

The team set up base camp at the glacier's lower terminus and assembled the
weather station. All eight researchers arrived safely by snowcat.

## 19 March — Ice Core Drilling Begins

The drilling crew extracted the first ice cores from the central plateau. The
cores were 40 metres deep and were sealed for transport to the laboratory.

## 02 April — Crevasse Survey Completed

The mapping crew finished charting the crevasse field on the eastern flank.
Three previously unrecorded crevasses were added to the regional map.

## 21 April — Meltwater Sampling

Water samples were collected from twelve meltwater channels to measure mineral
content. Sampling continued for a full week without interruption.

## 14 May — Wildlife Observations

The team recorded sightings of snow hares and a pair of ptarmigan near base
camp. These wildlife notes are kept separate from the glaciology results and
are considered incidental to the expedition's scientific goals.

## 30 May — Base Camp Decommissioned

The team packed the weather station, removed all equipment, and departed the
glacier, leaving the site clean. The expedition formally concluded.
