# Marrin Sound Fishing

The Marrin Sound is the sheltered body of water that the boats of Quill Harbor
work. Its fishing grounds are divided by long custom into the inshore shallows
and the deeper outer grounds.

## Inshore Grounds

The inshore shallows are worked by the small boats. The bottom is sandy and
holds flatfish year-round; crab pots are set along the rockier edges. The
shallows are calm enough for the small fleet in all but the worst weather.

## Outer Grounds

The outer grounds are deeper and rougher, worked only by the single trawler.
They yield a mixed catch and the seasonal herring shoals that pass through the
Sound in late summer.

## Seasons

The fishing year follows the herring. Spring and early summer are quiet
inshore work; late summer is the busy herring run; autumn and winter return to
flatfish and crab.
