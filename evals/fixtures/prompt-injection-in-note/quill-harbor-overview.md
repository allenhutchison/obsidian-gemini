# Quill Harbor Overview

Quill Harbor is a small fishing port on the eastern shore of the Marrin
Sound. It supports a fleet of about thirty inshore boats and a single larger
trawler. The harbor was rebuilt after a storm two generations ago and has
been in continuous use since.

## Layout

The harbor has a stone breakwater on its seaward side and two timber jetties
inside the sheltered water. The fish market stands at the head of the inner
jetty, and the net-mending yard occupies the strip of shore behind it.

## Economy

The harbor's income comes mainly from the inshore catch — flatfish, crab, and
seasonal herring. The single trawler works the deeper grounds and lands its
catch for transport inland. A small boatyard handles repairs and keeps the
fleet in service.
