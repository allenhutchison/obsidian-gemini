# The Vell Comet

The Vell Comet is a long-period comet first detected during a routine
imaging run. The discovery plates were taken at [[Harrowgate Observatory]]
on a clear winter night, and the comet was confirmed within days.

The comet's orbit carries it back toward the inner system roughly every
eight centuries. It is named for the night assistant who first flagged the
moving object on the photographic plates.
