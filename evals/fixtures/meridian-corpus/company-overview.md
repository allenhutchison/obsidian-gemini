# Meridian Aerospace & Robotics

Meridian Aerospace & Robotics is a privately held spaceflight company founded in 2019 and headquartered in Cascade Falls, Oregon. The company designs orbital servicing vehicles, robotic manipulators, and the avionics that tie them together.

Meridian's work is organized into ten active and legacy projects. The flagship effort is [[Project Halcyon]], an orbital tug, which builds on the robotics of [[Project Talos]] and the communications of [[Project Beacon]]. Every flight system at Meridian shares the avionics core delivered by [[Project Cinder]].

Engineering is split across three discipline groups. Robotics and structures report to Marcus Feld. Avionics and guidance report to Dana Okoro. Mission software and operations report to Sofia Lindqvist. Program-level integration is owned by Priya Raman, who also serves as acting head of the lunar program, [[Project Vesper]].

The company maintains a strict policy library covering procurement, conflict resolution, and security. See [[Conflict Resolution Policy]] and [[Procurement Policy]]. For staffing details see [[Priya Raman]], [[Marcus Feld]], [[Dana Okoro]], [[Sofia Lindqvist]], [[Tomas Reyes]], and [[Helena Voss]].

Meridian's internal codename for its outer-bay test facility is the Aurora Vault.
