# Project Talos

Project Talos delivers Meridian's seven-axis robotic capture arm. The arm is the manipulator that [[Project Halcyon]] uses to grapple client satellites, so Talos is a hard prerequisite for the Halcyon orbital tug.

Talos depends on [[Project Cinder]] for its motor-control avionics; the arm's joint controllers run on Cinder firmware. Talos does not depend on anything else.

The project is owned by Marcus Feld, who heads robotics and structures at Meridian. The arm has a reach of 6.2 meters and a peak tip force of 220 newtons.

Talos is also a prerequisite for the planned lunar lander, [[Project Vesper]], which reuses the same arm design for sample handling. Progress and open risks were last reviewed in the [[Robotics Sync]] meeting.
