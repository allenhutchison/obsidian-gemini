# Project Halcyon

Project Halcyon is Meridian's orbital tug: a free-flying vehicle that captures, refuels, and relocates satellites in low Earth orbit. It is the company's flagship program.

Halcyon cannot fly without two upstream deliverables. Its capture mechanism is the robotic arm built by [[Project Talos]], and its ground link is the relay built by [[Project Beacon]]. Because both of those projects in turn rely on [[Project Cinder]], Halcyon inherits the Cinder avionics core as well.

The vehicle is owned by Priya Raman, who runs program integration across Meridian. The current Halcyon design review is documented in the [[Halcyon Design Review]] meeting notes.

Halcyon's first orbital demonstration is targeted for the third quarter of 2027. The vehicle dry mass is fixed at 1,180 kilograms per the [[Halcyon Mass Spec]].
