# Project Garnet

Project Garnet is Meridian's planned propulsion test program: a ground test stand and a small green-propellant thruster intended to qualify the engine for future servicing vehicles. It carries the largest budget of any Meridian project.

Garnet depends on [[Project Aster]], because the test stand needs the Aster star tracker for precise thrust-vector pointing. Through Aster, Garnet transitively depends on [[Project Cinder]]. Garnet has no other direct dependencies.

The program is owned by Tomas Reyes, the propulsion lead. Garnet's thruster targets 440 newtons of vacuum thrust.

Garnet is still in the planning phase; no test article has been fabricated. The stand design was reviewed in the [[Propulsion Stand Review]] meeting.
