# Project Vesper

Project Vesper is Meridian's planned lunar lander, intended to deliver small payloads to the lunar surface beginning in 2029. It is still in the planning phase and has no flight hardware.

Vesper reuses two earlier systems. It inherits the orbital-tug bus architecture from [[Project Halcyon]], and it reuses the robotic arm from [[Project Talos]] for sample handling. Through those two parents, Vesper transitively depends on [[Project Cinder]] as well.

Vesper is owned by Priya Raman, who serves as acting head of the lunar program. The lander's target landed mass is 480 kilograms.

Because Vesper is planned rather than active, it has no committed launch contract. Early concept work was discussed in the [[Lunar Concept Review]] meeting.
