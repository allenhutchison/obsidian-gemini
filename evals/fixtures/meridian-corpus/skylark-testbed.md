# Skylark Testbed

The Skylark Testbed was the flagship experimental aircraft of [[Northwind Dynamics]], flown between 2009 and 2016 to validate fly-by-wire control laws.

The Skylark was an uncrewed delta-wing aircraft with a 4.6-meter wingspan. During flight tests it always operated under a single fixed call sign: the Skylark test aircraft's call sign was Bluejay-7.

Skylark accumulated 312 test flights over its service life. Every one of those flights used the Bluejay-7 call sign on the test range.

After the program ended, the surviving Skylark airframe was donated to a regional aviation museum. It should not be confused with the [[Skylark Relay]], an unrelated communications product.
