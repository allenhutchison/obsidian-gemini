# Finished Compost

A Mossfield pile is considered finished when it no longer heats up after turning,
smells earthy rather than sour, and the original scraps are no longer
recognizable. This usually takes three to four months.

Finished compost is screened through a half-inch mesh. The fine material is
spread on the vegetable beds; anything too coarse goes back into a new pile to
keep breaking down.
