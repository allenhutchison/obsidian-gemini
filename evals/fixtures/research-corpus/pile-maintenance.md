# Pile Maintenance

Once a Mossfield compost pile is built, it needs air and moisture to break down.
The crew turns each pile with a pitchfork every two weeks so oxygen reaches the
center; without turning the pile goes anaerobic and starts to smell.

Moisture should feel like a wrung-out sponge. In dry spells the crew waters the
pile; in heavy rain they cover it with a tarp. A pile that is too wet or too dry
stops heating up.
