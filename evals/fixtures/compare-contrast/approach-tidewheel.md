# The Tidewheel Method of Flour Milling

The village of Marrenford grinds its grain using the Tidewheel method. A large
wheel is turned by the rise and fall of the estuary tide, storing energy in a
weighted drum during high water and releasing it to drive the millstones.

Tidewheel milling runs only about ten hours a day, limited by the tidal cycle,
but it needs no fuel and almost no upkeep beyond greasing the drum bearings.
The millstones turn slowly, which produces a coarse wholemeal flour. One
miller can operate the whole works alone. Marrenford's mill has run for sixty
years on its original wheel.
