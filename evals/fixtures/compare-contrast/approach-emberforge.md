# The Emberforge Method of Flour Milling

The town of Caldshaw grinds its grain using the Emberforge method. A coal-fired
boiler drives a steam engine that spins the millstones at high speed, and the
mill can operate around the clock regardless of weather or tide.

Emberforge milling produces a fine, evenly ground white flour and can process
far more grain per day than a tidal mill. However, it consumes a steady supply
of coal, requires a crew of four — including a dedicated boiler stoker — and
the boiler must be inspected and overhauled every season. Caldshaw rebuilt its
engine twice in twenty years.
