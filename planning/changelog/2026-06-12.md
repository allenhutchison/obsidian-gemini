### [#978 feat: auto-dev skill — autonomous issue-to-PR pipeline](https://github.com/allenhutchison/obsidian-gemini/pull/978)

Introduces the **auto-dev** skill: an autonomous pipeline that runs one tick per invocation of a label-driven state machine (`auto:needs-info` → `auto:planned` → `auto:ready` → `auto:in-progress`). Each tick triages open issues, posts implementation plans for maintainer approval, builds the oldest approved issue into a PR, and addresses review feedback — with two human-only gates: plan approval and merge.

Ships with `scripts/auto-dev.sh` (dedicated clone, PID lockfile, per-tick spend cap and wall-clock watchdog), `scripts/auto-dev-settings.json` (curated allowlist that hard-denies merges, releases, force pushes, and direct pushes to master), and a new `AGENTS.md` section documenting the label state machine, comment-marker convention, and maintainer-side interaction workflow. A dry-run against the live repo validated the triage logic before merge.
