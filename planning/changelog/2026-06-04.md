### [#932 refactor: type AgentView confirmation/result params with Tool and ToolResult](https://github.com/allenhutchison/obsidian-gemini/pull/932)

Architecture-audit sweep. `AgentView.showConfirmationInChat` declared `tool: any` even though the `IConfirmationProvider` interface in `src/tools/types.ts` already uses `tool: Tool`. Matching `SendContext.showConfirmationInChat` had the same issue. While there, `showToolResult` accepted `result: any` but its delegate was already typed `ToolResult`. Both sites are now strongly typed. Pure type-only change.

### [#931 refactor: extract RagProgressProvider interface to replace 3 `progressProvider: any` sites](https://github.com/allenhutchison/obsidian-gemini/pull/931)

Architecture-audit sweep. `RagVaultScanner` accepted `progressProvider: any` on three methods and passed it straight through to `RagProgressModal`, whose constructor had the real four-method shape inlined. That shape is now named `RagProgressProvider` in `rag-types.ts` and imported at all three scanner sites and in the modal. Pure type tightening, no behavior change.

### [#930 fix: agent-view session-title rename calls non-existent sanitizeFileName method](https://github.com/allenhutchison/obsidian-gemini/pull/930)

Architecture-audit sweep caught an `as any` cast masking a latent bug. The double-click title-rename in `AgentViewUI` was casting `sessionManager` to `any` to call `sanitizeFileName`, but `SessionManager` never exposed that method — it lives in `src/utils/file-utils.ts`. The cast resolved to `undefined` at runtime, causing `TypeError: undefined is not a function` that the surrounding try/catch swallowed. Session-title renames silently no-op'd. Importing the utility directly removes the cast and fixes the rename.
